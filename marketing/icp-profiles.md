# FieldNotes — ICP Profiles

## Primary ICP

### Profile 1: The Frustrated Owner-Operator
**Who:** Runs the business AND works in the field
**Business:** 1-5 employees, service-based
**Revenue:** $150K-500K/yr
**Current tools:** Text messages, memory, maybe QuickBooks
**Pain:** Can't be everywhere at once. When they're working, they have no visibility. When they're in the office, productivity drops.
**Motivation:** Wants to scale without being on every job site
**Decision trigger:** Missed a $5K follow-up because nobody flagged it

### Profile 2: The Growing Crew Owner
**Who:** Owner is mostly off the tools, managing teams
**Business:** 5-15 employees, multiple trucks/crews
**Revenue:** $500K-$2M/yr
**Current tools:** Jobber or Housecall Pro (that workers ignore), whiteboard, texts
**Pain:** Has software but workers won't use it. End-of-day debriefs are incomplete.
**Motivation:** Professionalize operations. Get real data on field performance.
**Decision trigger:** Lost a big client due to poor communication/documentation

### Profile 3: The "Texts Are My CRM" Operator
**Who:** Solo or 1-2 helpers, been doing it for years
**Business:** 1-3 people, referral-based
**Revenue:** $80K-200K/yr
**Current tools:** Text messages, paper notepad, "I'll remember"
**Pain:** Forgetting follow-ups. Losing track of supply needs. No record of service history.
**Motivation:** Get organized without complexity
**Decision trigger:** A client said "you were supposed to do X" and they had no record

## Key Messaging by Vertical

### HVAC / Plumbing / Electrical
- "Emergency calls are your bread and butter. Don't let a follow-up slip because nobody wrote it down."
- "When Mrs. Johnson calls asking what you did last visit, you'll have the answer in 10 seconds."

### Landscaping / Lawn Care
- "Crews hit 15-20 properties a day. Which ones needed extra work? Which ones asked for a quote on new services?"
- "End of season: which clients asked about fall cleanups? You'll know because it's in the system."

### Commercial Cleaning
- "Night crews. Zero supervision. Now you get a summary every morning of what happened."
- "Supply room running low? Your workers tell the bot, not their memory."

### Pest Control
- "Recurring service = recurring notes. Which treatments were applied? Any new activity? All logged automatically."
- "Regulatory compliance: service records on demand. No more digging through paper."
